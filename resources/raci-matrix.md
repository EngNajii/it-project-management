# 👥 RACI Matrix Guide

A RACI matrix clarifies who does what on a project. No more "I thought you were handling that."

---

## What Does RACI Stand For?

| Letter | Role | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|
| **R** | Responsible | Does the work. Can be multiple people. |
| **A** | Accountable | Owns the outcome. Signs off. **Only ONE per task.** |
| **C** | Consulted | Provides input before decisions are made. Two-way communication. |
| **I** | Informed | Kept up to date. One-way communication (FYI only). |

---

## RACI Matrix Template

*Add your tasks/deliverables in rows. Add roles/people in columns. Fill in R, A, C, or I.*

| Task / Deliverable | PM | Tech Lead | Dev Team | QA | Business Owner | Sponsor |
|--------------------|----|-----------|----------|----|---------------|---------|
| Project Charter | A | C | I | I | C | A |
| Requirements Gathering | R | C | C | C | R | I |
| Architecture Design | C | A | R | I | I | I |
| Development | I | A | R | C | I | I |
| Testing & QA | C | C | C | A/R | R | I |
| UAT Sign-off | C | I | I | I | R | A |
| Go-Live Approval | R | C | I | I | C | A |
| Post-Launch Review | A/R | R | R | R | C | I |

---

## Rules for a Good RACI

1. **Every task must have exactly ONE Accountable (A)**
2. **Every task must have at least one Responsible (R)**
3. **Avoid overloading one person** – if someone has R on everything, it's a red flag
4. **Minimize Consulted (C)** – too many C's slow decisions down
5. **Keep Informed (I) relevant** – don't inform everyone about everything

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Two people marked A | Confusion about who owns the decision | Pick one. The other becomes C or R. |
| No one marked R | Work won't get done | Assign at least one person to do the task |
| Everyone is C | Decision paralysis | Limit C to those truly needed before decisions |
| PM is A for everything | PM is a bottleneck | Delegate accountability to appropriate owners |

---

## How to Facilitate a RACI Workshop

1. List all project deliverables and key tasks (20–40 items is typical)
2. List all project roles (not names — roles)
3. For each task, ask: *"Who is doing the work?" (R) → "Who owns the outcome?" (A)*
4. Fill in C and I last
5. Review row by row: does each task have exactly one A?
6. Review column by column: is any role overloaded?
7. Get sign-off from stakeholders

---

> 💡 **Tip:** Build your RACI during project planning, not after problems arise.
